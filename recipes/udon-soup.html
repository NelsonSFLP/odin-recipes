Udon Soup

Description

Udon soup is a beloved Japanese culinary delight that has a rich history and holds a significant place in Japanese cuisine and culture. Originating in the 9th century in Japan, udon noodles are thick and chewy wheat noodles that have become a staple in Japanese cooking. Udon soup, with its comforting flavors and satisfying texture, has gained popularity both in Japan and worldwide. Traditionally, udon soup is prepared by simmering udon noodles in a flavorful broth made from ingredients such as soy sauce, mirin, and dashi, a traditional Japanese stock. The toppings and garnishes vary, offering a wide range of options to customize the soup to individual preferences. Common additions include green onions, sliced mushrooms, tempura, tofu, and nori, providing a burst of flavors and textures to the dish.

Udon soup not only satisfies the taste buds but also reflects the cultural significance of communal dining in Japan. It is often enjoyed in noodle shops, where people gather to savor this warm and hearty dish together. Udon noodles are considered a comfort food, perfect for colder months or any time when a fulfilling and nourishing meal is desired. Today, udon soup has gained popularity worldwide, becoming a staple in Japanese restaurants and attracting food enthusiasts seeking an authentic taste of Japan. Its versatility and ability to accommodate various dietary preferences, such as vegetarian or vegan options, have contributed to its widespread appeal. Overall, udon soup represents the harmonious blend of flavors, textures, and cultural heritage, offering a satisfying and memorable dining experience for both locals and visitors alike.

Ingredients

  • 1 litre Massel salt reduced chicken style liquid stock
  • 2 chicken breast fillets
  • 1/3 cup red miso paste
  • 180g dried udon noodles
  • 1 large carrot, diagonally sliced
  • 300g broccoli, small florets
  • 100g fresh shiitake mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup frozen peeled edamame
  • 300g silken firm tofu, cut into 3cm pieces
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Finely shredded nori, to serve
  • Toasted sesame seeds, to serve

Steps

  1. Place stock and 1 litre cold water in a large saucepan over high heat. Bring to the boil. Add chicken. Return to the boil. Reduce heat to low.
  2. Simmer, covered, for 15 minutes or until chicken is just cooked through. Remove from heat. Stand chicken in stock mixture for 5 minutes. Transfer chicken to a chopping board. Thinly slice chicken.
  3. Meanwhile, place miso paste and 1/4 cup of hot stock mixture in a small bowl. Stir until smooth.
  4. Cook noodles following packet directions. Drain.
  5. Meanwhile, bring stock mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat. Add paste mixture. Cook, stirring, for 1 minute or until combined. Add carrot and broccoli. Cook for 2 minutes. Add mushrooms, edamame, noodles, chicken and tofu. Cook for 1 minute or until heated through.
  6. Divide broth mixture among serving bowls. Top with onion, nori and sesame seeds. Serve.
